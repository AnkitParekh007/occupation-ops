# Rejection Diagnosis

## 1. Why the Old Resume Was Likely Rejected

The old resume had strong experience behind it, but the presentation was hurting ATS and recruiter screening.

- It was too long at 6 PDF pages and included too much early-career detail.
- The title “Creative Front-end Programmer” sounded outdated for international Senior/Lead Frontend Engineer roles.
- The profile mentioned 10+ years, while the current positioning is 18+ years of web/frontend experience.
- Many bullets used weak phrasing such as “involved in,” which hides ownership and leadership.
- Leadership was not visible enough even though you have Team Lead, Front End Lead, Lead Front-end Programmer, and architecture responsibilities.
- The resume mixed too many old technologies, which made the profile look less focused.
- Angular, React, TypeScript, enterprise SaaS, architecture, PIM/PXM, component systems, and AI frontend positioning were not clearly surfaced.
- It included unnecessary personal details: DOB, marital status, mother tongue, hobbies, Facebook, and alternate phone numbers.
- Grammar, spacing, and spelling issues reduced credibility.
- Project sections were repetitive and difficult to scan.
- Metrics and business impact were mostly missing.
- AI/copilot/frontend automation work was not positioned in a credible, recruiter-friendly way.

## 2. What Was Fixed

- Created a modern 2-page-style Lead Frontend resume focused on Angular, React, TypeScript, enterprise SaaS, architecture, leadership, and delivery.
- Created a second AI Frontend resume that positions you as a frontend/product engineer building copilot UI, agent workspace UX, streaming UI, and enterprise automation workflows.
- Removed outdated personal details, hobbies, Facebook, school-level detail overload, and long legacy tool lists.
- Rewrote weak bullets with stronger ownership language: led, owned, built, delivered, partnered, improved, supported, resolved.
- Consolidated early-career roles so recruiters see the senior experience first.
- Added clear target titles and ATS-friendly sections.
- Highlighted leadership, team sizes, product platforms, UAT, production support, API integration, and architecture ownership.
- Added placeholders for measurable impact where numbers are not yet confirmed.
- Created LinkedIn positioning, recruiter messages, and a tailoring guide for different job types.

- “Led frontend delivery for [X] modules across [Y] enterprise product workflows.”
- “Guided a team of [X] developers across [Y]-year product delivery.”
- “Supported [X] enterprise customers / business units / product teams.”
- “Reduced recurring UI defects by [X%] through reusable validation and component patterns.”
